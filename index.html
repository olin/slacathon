SLACATHON

March 22, 2014, Olin Milas Hall

What is SLACathon?


SLACathon is Olin College’s first official design, hacking, and making marathon, hosted by Olin’s dedicated Stay Late And Create (SLAC) student organization. SLAC is passionate about providing opportunities for people to come together and fostering new ideas and projects.

In the spirit of SLAC, this first SLACathon will be focused on building communities. There is no specific theme, but there will be broad criteria that will encompass multidiscplinary projects

125 students from Olin College, Babson College, and Wellesley University will combine their skills and passion on March 28th to create awesome projects, and we would love for you to be a part of this experience.

What makes SLACathon special?


The combined engineering, business, and liberal arts expertise from the three schools of the BOW collaboration - Babson, Olin, and Wellesley - creates the perfect atmosphere for ideas to grow. SLACathon embodies the multidisciplinary approach of the BOW collaboration itself - and plays perfectly into our theme of building community.

With teams of Babson, Olin, and Wellesley students and other members of the community, SLACathon is focused on gathering knowledge and enthusiasm to breathe life into incredible ideas.

FAQ


Who can attend the event?

SLACathon is open to all. We encourage Babson, Olin, and Wellesley (BOW) students to attend, so BOW students will have a higher priority during registration!

What should I bring with me?

All attendees are required to bring any form of valid ID for admission (name must match registration) and any basic tools for creating. We may provide additional hardware to hack on during the event.

How much does attending cost?

Admission is free, and includes food, drinks, and miscellaneous swag for the entire SLACathon!

What food will you provide?

We will have food and drinks throughout the event. Anything from waffles to pizza and massive supply of fruits.

What is SLACathon?


SLACathon@Olin is Olin College’s first official design, hacking, and maker marathon. hosted by Olin’s dedicated Stay Late And Create (SLAC) student organization. SLAC is passionate about providing opportunity for people to come together to foster new ideas and projects.

Encompassing more than conventional hacking, SLACathon @Olin aims to bring together diverse flavors of skills and experiences to create. In the spirit of SLAC, this first SLACathon will be focused on community building.

75 Olin College and 50 Wellesley University and Babson College students will be combining their skills and passion on March 22nd to create awesome projects, and we would love for you to be a part of this experience.

FAQ


Who can attend the event?

All undergraduate students from any university are welcome. Non-BOW students must be 18 or older. Unfortunately, high school students and graduate students cannot attend.

What should I bring with me?

All attendees are required to bring their valid student ID for admission (name must match registration) and a laptop for hacking. For hardware hacks, we will have hardware for you to hack on.

How much does attending cost?

Admission is free, and includes food, drinks, and miscellaneous swag for the entire hackathon!

What food will you provide?

We will have food and drinks throughout the event. Anything from waffles to pizza and massive supply of fruits.
